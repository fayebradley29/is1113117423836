Why are Cloud Services Interesting?

Cloud computing is everywhere, but surprisingly few people understand exactly what it is, or the incredible power it has to continue to revolutionize our digital landscape.

One of the most interesting facts about cloud computing is that data storage doesn’t actually take place in some nebulous ether like many imagine it does. Cloud computing simply means that your data is stored on the Internet using remote servers, rather than being stored locally on your hard drive or company network. It's your data stored in another location. This type of remote storage is like a cloud, however, in the sense that your data essentially follows you everywhere, enabling you to retrieve and use information anywhere in the world.



There Are Multiple Formats of Cloud.

Depending on your business’s IT support needs, you can get many different types of cloud computing applications. You might, for example, choose a private solution, where your data is externally hosted on servers that are dedicated solely to your company. It can also be cost effective to share space with others, though, in which case you might want to go with community hosting. If you want to offer the public space on your servers, choose a public solution, or a hybrid of multiple types. If you aren't ready to switch all of your storage and processes to the cloud, you can use cloud applications to gain some of the benefits, like Office 365.
